Hartselle Historic Facts

Founded in 1870, Hartselle, Alabama has a long history and proud traditions. Affectionately known as “The City of Southern Hospitality,” Hartselle has a rich history with families risiding here that go back generations. The city was placed strategically alongside the South and North Alabama Railroad making it a railroad town. Hartselle was named after founding father George Hartselle. Some of his descendants remain in Hartselle. In 1904 a disastrous fire destroyed the entire downtown district along the tracks on Railroad Street. Hartselle was rebuilt on what is currently the historic business district on Main Street that runs perpindicular to the tracks.

Hartselle Current Demographics

Hartselle is a city located in Morgan County Alabama. With a 2020 population of 14,546, it is the 45th largest city in Alabama and the 2366th largest city in the United States. Hartselle is currently growing at a rate of 0.28% annually and its population has increased by 2.04% since the most recent census, which recorded a population of 14,255 in 2010. Hartselle has reached it's highest recorded population. Spanning over 17 miles, Hartselle has a population density of 885 people per square mile.
The average household income in Hartselle is $76,588 with a poverty rate of 8.48%. The median rental costs in recent years comes to $681 per month, and the median house value is $148,600. The median age in Hartselle is 41.6 years, 37.3 years for males, and 44.3 years for females.
