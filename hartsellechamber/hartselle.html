Welcome from Mayor Randy Garrison

On behalf of the City of Hartselle I welcome your interest in our hometown. Affectionately known as “The City of Southern Hospitality” we seek to share this with those who live here, as well as those who visit our fair city. Hartselle offers a highly rated school system, park and recreation facilities for many sports, an outstanding aquatic center, arts center, historical and contemporary housing, large number of churches for the faith based community, unique dining facilities and a safe place for raising your family. Hartselle is ideal for those looking for a small town lifestyle, but is also located conveniently to larger cities for other services. Whether you are coming to visit or planning to call Hartselle home, we welcome you and know you will find your place here among friends.

Freight House Restaurant

The Freight House Restaurant is a modern southern restaurant located in downtown Hartselle, Alabama. We are located in the historic freight terminal. This historic landmark has been remodeled as a dining facility and catering venue. Our guests may hear passing trains and witness a unique union of the past and present.

Hartselle Area Upcoming Events

Do you have a second-grade student in Hartselle? Send us their Letter to Santa and see it published in the newspaper! This special section will print Dec. 22 and will include letters and colorful artwork.
You can mail letters to the North Pole (c/o the Hartselle Enquirer) or drop them off at the office at 206 Cedar St. NW. Mailed letters should be delivered to P.O. Box 929, Hartselle, AL 35640 no later than Dec. 20.

First Christian Church in Hartselle, 209 E. Main St., presents a live nativity scene in front of the church. The annual event features live animals as well as members of the church dressed as Bible characters. The scene will be staged nightly Dec. 8-23 from 6-7:30 p.m. The public is invited to drive by or walk up to view and pet the animals. This will be the fourth year for the event, which organizers said is designed to draw attention to the true meaning of the season.
